SAS Music App

“The best music discovery algorithms”

Project background

SAS is an online streaming music website and tries to give great music experices to music lovers. Based on researches from their team we find out that their users need to use their smart devices to access to SAS website. So, they asked us to design a music app to help users have their music handy whenever they want it.

Users

Users include all people who love to listen to music specially on their smart devices.

Research findings

We collected all the feedbacks that we received from our research from users. We converted these feedbacks to user personas. One of them is Frez, a 25-year busy painter artist who needs fast access to her music files because she doesn’t enough free time to explore and search for them. The research revealed that Frez usually wants to have guick access to her music playlists. So she needs a good mobile music app.

The problem

Their website it's NOT a responsive website and need to converted to a mobile and/or tablet friendly version. Also they need an music app for all platforms.

Ideate & Design

We learned some useful things from our research and then we braistormed some solutions and converted them into designs.

Wireframes

Taking the time to create designs of each screen on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points and then we designed the very first screen based on user research.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

The low-fidelity prototype connected the primary user flow of listening and sharing music, so the prototype could be used in a usability study with users.

Research Plan & Usability Study

We conducted a usability study on first week of May 2021. It was a remote, unmoderated study with 10 participants. We needed to find out user pain points and their difficulties. We assigned different tasks and each one lasted for 5-10 minutes, including the interview questions. Participants were all music lovers (balanced number of male and female - 18-51 years old).

Usability study insights
After our study we figuired out these area need to be addressed:

  Almost all users were confused about home menu icon, so we need to change its location.
  Not everyone could find library menu items quickly, so they need more clues about library menu and all of its submenus.
  Some users didn’t understand the difference between free-version and the pro one, therefore they need more information about SAS Pro and its features.
Mockup
We changed many things after the usability studies. Such as adding dividers and buttons. Also we changed the whole background color and icons. Then we added some good looking boxes and changed the menu button style and its location.
High-Fidelity Prototype
The hi-fi prototype followed the same user flow as the lo-fi prototype, and included the design changes made after the usability study.
Accessibility considerations
in order to provide an inclusive and accessible experience for all we changed the whole background color and used larger texts for those users who are vision impaired. We used better icons with different style to help using app easier. Also, we used detailed imagery for category and library options to help users understand the designs.
